<p><p>The surrogacy service market comprises two main types: gestational surrogacy and traditional surrogacy. Gestational surrogacy involves an egg from the intended mother or a donor and sperm from the intended father, with the surrogate carrying the resulting embryo. Traditional surrogacy uses the surrogate's egg, making her the biological mother of the child. Both types serve individuals and couples facing fertility challenges, each offering distinct legal, emotional, and ethical considerations based on the surrogate's biological connection to the child.</p></p>
