<p><p>Additive manufacturing machines encompass various technologies for 3D printing. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) uses molten filament to create layers, known for affordability and ease of use. Stereolithography (SLA) employs UV light to cure resin into solid forms, achieving high precision. Digital Light Processing (DLP) utilizes a digital light projector for faster resin curing. Material Jetting (MJ) deposits droplets of material layer by layer, enabling multi-material printing. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) uses lasers to fuse powdered materials, ideal for durable parts. Other methods include Binder Jetting and Electron Beam Melting, each offering unique advantages for specific applications.</p></p>
