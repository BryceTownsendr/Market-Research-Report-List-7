<p><p>Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) systems enhance security by requiring multiple forms of verification. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) combines something you know (like a password) with something you have (like a smartphone). Three-Factor Authentication (3FA) adds biometric data to this mix, such as a fingerprint. Four-Factor and Five-Factor systems incorporate additional layers, like behavioral analytics or location verification, further strengthening security. As data breaches rise, these increasingly complex MFA types cater to varying security needs across industries.</p></p>
