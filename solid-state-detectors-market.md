<p><p>The solid-state detectors market primarily includes semiconductor detectors and diamond detectors. Semiconductor detectors utilize materials like silicon or germanium to convert radiation into electrical signals, offering high sensitivity and resolution, making them ideal for applications in medical imaging and particle detection. Diamond detectors, on the other hand, are known for their exceptional radiation hardness and thermal conductivity, making them suitable for high-radiation environments, such as particle accelerators and space applications. Both types contribute significantly to advancements in detection technologies.</p></p>
