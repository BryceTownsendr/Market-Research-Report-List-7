<p><p>The automotive multimeter market is primarily segmented into digital and analog types. Digital multimeters (DMMs) offer enhanced accuracy, user-friendly interfaces, and multiple measurement features, making them popular for modern automotive diagnostics. In contrast, analog multimeters display readings through a needle on a dial, providing real-time movement and continuous feedback, which some technicians prefer for specific applications. Both types serve essential roles in vehicle maintenance, but digital models are increasingly favored due to their advanced capabilities and precision.</p></p>
