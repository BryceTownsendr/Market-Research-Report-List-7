<p><p>Butyric acid, also known as butanoic acid, is a short-chain fatty acid with the chemical formula C4H8O2. It is a colorless liquid with a pungent odor, naturally found in animal fats, plant oils, and as a byproduct of fermentation in the human gut. Butyric acid is widely used in various industries, including food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, animal feed, and chemicals. In the food industry, it serves as a flavoring agent, while in animal feed, it is used as an additive to improve gut health and growth performance. In pharmaceuticals, it has potential applications in cancer treatment and anti-inflammatory therapies.</p><p>The Butyric Acid Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.8% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand in animal feed additives, rising awareness of gut health, and expanding applications in the pharmaceutical and chemical industries. The Asia-Pacific region is anticipated to dominate the market due to rapid industrialization, growing livestock production, and increasing demand for processed foods. Key trends include the development of bio-based butyric acid production methods to meet sustainability goals and the rising adoption of butyric acid in functional foods and dietary supplements. However, challenges such as fluctuating raw material prices and regulatory constraints may hinder market growth.</p></p>
