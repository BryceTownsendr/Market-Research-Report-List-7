<p><p>Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) is a nuclear imaging technique that allows for functional imaging of the body by detecting gamma rays emitted from a radiotracer introduced into the patient. Hospitals and diagnostic centers are adopting SPECT for its ability to provide detailed functional information complementing anatomical imaging techniques like CT and MRI.</p><p>Recent trends include the emergence of hybrid imaging modalities, such as SPECT/CT, enhancing diagnostic accuracy. There is also an increasing focus on radiopharmaceutical development, optimizing the specificity of tracers, and improving imaging resolution. Furthermore, the integration of artificial intelligence in image analysis is set to refine diagnostic capabilities.
