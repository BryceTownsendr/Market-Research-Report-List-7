<p><p>The powder metallurgy tool steels market consists primarily of two types: molybdenum-type and wolfram-type. Molybdenum-type tool steels, known for their excellent toughness and wear resistance, are commonly used in applications like cutting tools and molds. In contrast, wolfram-type tool steels, or tungsten-based steels, offer high hardness and thermal stability, making them suitable for high-speed tools. Both types are key to advancements in industrial tooling, catering to diverse manufacturing needs and enhancing operational efficiency.</p></p>
