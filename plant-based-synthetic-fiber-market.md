<p><p>The plant-based synthetic fiber market primarily comprises three types: PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate), PTT (Polytrimethylene Terephthalate), and PLA (Polylactic Acid). PET is derived from renewable resources, enhancing its sustainability compared to traditional petrochemical fibers. PTT offers better elasticity and durability, making it ideal for textiles and various applications. PLA is made from fermented plant starch, providing biodegradable options for environmentally conscious consumers. Together, these fibers are driving innovation and sustainability in the textile industry, addressing eco-friendly demands.</p></p>
