<p><p>The rotary tiller market primarily consists of front-tine and rear-tine tillers, each serving distinct gardening needs. Front-tine tillers have tines positioned at the front, making them ideal for lighter soil and smaller gardens, allowing for easy maneuverability. Conversely, rear-tine tillers feature tines located at the rear, offering greater power and stability, suitable for larger areas and tougher soil conditions. Overall, the choice between these types depends on garden size, soil type, and user convenience.</p></p>
