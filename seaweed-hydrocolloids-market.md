<p><p>The seaweed hydrocolloids market consists primarily of three types: agar-agar, alginates, and carrageenan. Agar-agar is derived from red algae and is widely used as a gelling agent in food and laboratory applications. Alginates, sourced from brown seaweed, serve as thickening and stabilizing agents in various industries, including food and pharmaceuticals. Carrageenan, also obtained from red seaweed, is primarily utilized in the food sector for its emulsifying and thickening properties, enhancing texture and mouthfeel in a range of products.</p></p>
